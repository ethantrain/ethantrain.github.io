European Cantaloupe

The European cantaloupes appearence is quite different from that of the North American cataloupe. The European cantaloupe is lightly ribbed with a sweet and flavorful flesh and a gray-green skin.

Cantaloupe

Cantaloupe is a variety of the Cucumis melo species in the Cucurbitaceae family. Cantaloupes range in weight from 1.1 to 11 pounds. At first cantaloupe only referred to the non-netted, orange-fleshed melons of Europe.

North American cantaloupe

The North American Cantaloupe is most common in the United States, Mexico, and some parts of Canada. It is also known as a muskmelon. This cantaloupe has a "net-like" skin covering. It is a round melon with firm, orange, moderately sweet flesh and a thin, light-grey rind.
